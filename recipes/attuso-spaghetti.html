Attuso Spaghetti

Desciption

This is perhaps one of my favorite recipes to make when it comes to connecting with my familial roots. My great grandmother would make this, which she learned from her Sicilian parents. Their original recipe was much different. It would encompass every left over meat remaining in the house. The sauce would be kept in the refrigerator, or on the stove at a low simmer, for days. By the end of the week, the spaghetti would contain everything from ground beef to chicken to even pork chops.

However, I add a little Cajun flare to it by using homemade meatballs with italian sausage, or andouille if that is what I have leftover. The meatball recipe comes from my grandfather on another side of the family. I learned to combine these two recipes from him. They go every well together and are served best with parmesian and perhaps some garlic bread.

Ingredients

Sauce

  • Oil
  • Yellow Onion
  • Tomato Paste
  • Celery
  • Garlic
  • Tomato Sauce
  • Parmesean Cheese

Meatballs

  • Ground Beef
  • Ground Italian Sausage
  • Salt
  • Pepper
  • Lemon Zest
  • Allspice
  • Parmesean
  • Egg
  • Bread
  • Milk

Steps

Sauce

  1. Heat a small amount of oil in the pot. Add onion and cook until translucent.
  2. Add tomato paste and combine with a little water to prevent sticking. Cook this for only 3 or 4 minutes. Careful not to stick!
  3. Add celery and cook until soft. Then add garlic and cook until fragrant. No more than a few minutes.
  4. Once all ingredients are combined. Add canned tomato sauce with equal parts water and bring to boil. Add water when needed to keep consistant desired thickness.

Meatballs

  1. Add milk to a bowl and torn chunks of bread. Soak for 5 minutes then drain milk.
  2. Add all ingredients to bowl and combine thoroughly.
  3. Form into 1 inch balls and place on pan with baking sheet. Place in fridge for 1 hour to harden.
  4. Bake in oven for 20 minutes. Then, flip and bake for 10 minutes.
  5. Drain each meatball then place into sauce. Allow to soak sauce up before serving.
