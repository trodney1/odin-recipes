Crawfish Etouffee

Description

This is perhaps one of the most traditional and classic dishes of the Cajun cuisine. It has fed my family countless times and is so wholesome that you can't help but sit around the table or a fire and eat as a family.

Ingredients

  • Butter
  • Yellow Onion
  • Bell Pepper
  • Garlic
  • Boiled Crawfish
  • Spices

Steps

  1. Bring butter to a simmer and add onion. Cover with lid of pot. Etouffee means "smothered onions" in Cajun French. You want them to be soft and translucent.
  2. Add bell pepper and garlic. Cook until soft and garlic is fragrant.
  3. Add crawfish anc cook until warm.
  4. Season with your favorites, however, Tony's is the way to go!
  5. Serve on rice sprinkled with parsley on top.
